The Positioning

A third path between charter and full ownership.

Charter gives flexibility but often comes with high premiums. Full ownership gives control but requires significant capital, management, and fixed costs. AVIONA is designed as a structured ownership-access platform that combines aviation assets, usage rights, and investor economics in a more efficient model.

Charter

Flexible, but premium-priced.

Traditional charter solves access, but not ownership, transparency, or long-term participation in aircraft economics.

Full Ownership

Control, but capital intensive.

Aircraft ownership can provide control, but also brings acquisition cost, management complexity, fixed expenses, and utilization risk.

AVIONA

Ownership-access, structured for the digital era.

AVIONA uses a regulated fund framework and Web3-enabled ownership infrastructure to make private aviation more divisible, transparent, and usable.

The Model

Real asset. Regulated structure. Web3 experience.

The model can be understood through three layers. Each layer has a distinct role: asset ownership, legal participation, and digital interaction.

1

Asset Layer

Aircraft are held through a regulated fund or SPV framework, supported by professional aviation management and operating records.

2

Securities Layer

Participation is documented through defined fund, offering, governance, economic, and usage-right materials.

3

Web3 Layer

Tokenized records support a digital ownership experience for viewing holdings, allocation, and usage rights.

The Aircraft

Bombardier Global 5000 / 5500 class.

The initial aircraft platform is a long-range, large-cabin business jet category designed for intercontinental reach, comfort, and reliability from Hong Kong and key Asia-Pacific hubs.

Range

Approximately 10,500 km, with up to approximately 11 hours of flight endurance.

Cabin

15-seat configuration, forward galley, two washrooms, three-cabin layout, and spacious luggage capacity.

Use cases

Designed for business efficiency, family travel, lifestyle access, and executive hospitality.
